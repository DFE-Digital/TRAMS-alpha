Overall Financial and Governance Summary

The trust reported a cumulative surplus of £29,371k in its 2020/21 accounts, 22.5% of total income, which is a healthy level.

  • The cumulative surplus is forecast to be stable or to increase over the next three years (remaining above 5% of income)
  • The forecast surplus of £37,595k (25% of income) for 2023/24 is deemed by the ESFA as resilient to unexpected expenditure or a reduction in income if pupil numbers are not at the levels projected
  • There is a minimal risk of short‑term financial stress

The FS notes that the trust is undertaking due-diligence on a large secondary sponsor-led academy, that will potentially join the trust in February 2022. Work continues to secure the opening of five schools approved in Wave 13 and 14 of the free schools’ programme – Salford, Rochdale, Radcliffe, Wolverhampton and Manchester; although no target dates have yet been agreed. The trust is also preparing, in partnership with Eton College, for the submission of applications to open three selective sixth form colleges in the state sector within the expected Wave 15 of the free schools’ programme. The Trust has also continued discussions regarding the future sponsorship of further academies.
